Welcome!

Welcome to my home! Or to be more precise, a virtual archive of the different ways I define home

Having left my home country Colombia at 16 years old to study in an international school in Japan, I have spent the past couple of years of my life trying to understand where I fit in. How much time do you have to spend in a place to become part of a community? What responsibilities do I have towards the countries that I have visited or lived in? How do I incorporate what I have learned in different places into my daily life, wherever I am? In simple words, where is home? These are some of the questions that inspired me to build this website and that I hope you will explore with me while you visit my virtual house.

The structure of the website

Each page represents a country or region to which I think I have a meaningful connection. Even though determining what constitutes a meaningful connection is a complicated process full of ethical and personal considerations, I designed each room to represent a place in which I have spent more than two months and that transformed me, one way or another. Thus, there are five rooms that represent the regions of the world I have either visited or lived in during the last few years, plus a lobby that explains more in-depth how I define home and what inspired me to build this project.

As you explore each room, you will find four main sections: a short explanation of my general experience, a gallery of artifacts and objects (each with its own story because as Eduardo Galeano said “the world is not made of atoms, the world is made of stories”) and a Spotify playlist that I made during my time in each country.

So once again, welcome to my house. Feel free to explore the website, either following your intuition or jumping from one room to another in chronological order.
